SANAD — 4-MINUTE PITCH SCRIPT (English)
Structure: OFFICIAL TEMPLATE — Guy Kawasaki 10 slides (Enactus)
+ action-oriented conclusion slide, demo inside Slide 4
Format: 4 min pitch + 4 min Q&A · ~30-second demo max
================================================================
Workshop tips baked in: story hook, no superlatives, competition
acknowledged, risks addressed, measured-vs-estimated numbers.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 1 — TITLE  (0:00 – 0:10)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: SANAD logo · team names & roles · email · phone.
SAY:
"Good afternoon. We are [names], and this is SANAD — insurance
for Tunisia: verified in five minutes, declared in three."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 2 — PROBLEM / OPPORTUNITY  (0:10 – 0:40)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: the Sfax driver photo + 3 pain icons.
SAY:
"Last month, a driver in Sfax dented a bumper. It took him three
weeks and two agency visits to get an answer. His story is the
market in miniature: KYC is paper-based and redone at every
insurer; declaring a claim means fifteen to twenty minutes of
forms then days of processing, office hours only; and the forms
are in French while the client's real language is derja. Insurers
lose money on every small claim. Honest clients wait."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 3 — VALUE PROPOSITION  (0:40 – 1:00)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: one sentence + the two-pillar visual.
SAY:
"SANAD relieves that pain with one platform for the full journey:
verify your identity once — reusable across insurers — then
declare claims 24/7 in French, Arabic or derja, and get a
decision on the spot. For insurers: lower processing costs. For
clients: minutes instead of weeks, in their own language."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 4 — UNDERLYING MAGIC + DEMO  (1:00 – 1:55)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: the 3-gates schema (amount / AI confidence / fraud) —
figures, not text. Then LIVE DEMO.
SAY (15s):
"Our secret ingredient is a rule: the AI may only auto-approve a
claim when three lights are green — small amount, high
confidence, low fraud risk. Otherwise a human decides, and every
decision is stored with its reasons. Identity documents are
processed locally — they never leave the server. Here it is,
live."

DEMO (30 seconds, pre-loaded, rehearsed):
  1. Derja message — "sadamt fi 9alb el mdina" — bot replies in
     derja, claim fields fill in.
  2. Damage photo → repair estimate in dinars.
  3. THE MONEY SHOT: decision banner — green "auto-approved" or
     amber "routed to a human expert", with the reason.

SAY (10s):
"Automation when the AI is sure. A human when it doubts. An audit
trail a regulator can read."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 5 — BUSINESS MODEL  (1:55 – 2:15)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: who pays / channel / pricing, 3 bullets.
SAY:
"Who pays: insurers. How: SaaS — a fee per verified identity and
a fee per processed claim, plus a platform subscription. The
reusable KYC is the flywheel: each insurer that joins makes every
verified identity more valuable to the next one."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 6 — GO-TO-MARKET  (2:15 – 2:35)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: 3-step arrow: 1 pilot → auto claims < 500 TND → market.
SAY:
"We don't sell a platform, we sell a pilot: one Tunisian insurer,
small auto claims under 500 dinars — highest volume, safest
automation. Distribution is direct B2B: the Tunisian insurance
market is roughly twenty companies — you can meet them all. The
pilot's measured results become our sales deck."

----------------------------------------------------------------

SAY:
"Tractable estimates vehicle damage; Shift Technology detects

enterprise-priced, without derja. Clients choose SANAD for the
full journey, identity data that can stay in-country, Tunisian
law built in, and a price sized for this market."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 8 — TEAM  (2:55 – 3:05)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: photos, names, roles; advisors/partners if any.
SAY:
"We are [names/roles]. In 48 hours we shipped the working

SLIDE 9 — FINANCIAL FORECASTS & KEY METRICS  (3:05 – 3:30)
----------------------------------------------------------------
ON SLIDE: small table, clearly titled "Working hypotheses —
to be validated by the pilot":
  · Year 1: 1 pilot insurer · Year 3: 4-6 insurers (of ~20 in TN)
  · Volume: from ~10k verified identities and ~5k claims (Y1)
    to ~100k identities / 50k claims cumulative by Year 5
  · Pricing hypothesis: per-identity + per-claim fees in the
    single-digit-dinar range + platform subscription
  · Unit economics driver: -25 to -30% claim processing cost
    (formula: agent time × hourly rate × % auto-approved)
  · IRR: computed after pilot calibration — we will not invent it
SAY:
"Our numbers come in two kinds. Measured: minus eighty percent
declaration time, five-minute KYC, forty-six green tests.
Forecast — and the slide says 'hypotheses' on purpose: one pilot
insurer in year one, four to six by year three, with per-identity
and per-claim pricing. We deliberately do not show an IRR today:
it would be fiction before the pilot calibrates the auto-approval
rate. That honesty is our method."

Q&A PREPARATION (4 minutes — anticipated questions)
================================================================

Q: "Too ambitious for a hackathon — what actually works?"
A: "Everything we demoed runs end-to-end: Arabic ID OCR, AML
   screening, liveness, derja chat, photo damage estimate, gated
   decision, database persistence. 46 automated tests are green.
   What is NOT in the MVP is deliberately listed: insurer system
   integration, real payouts, complex bodily-injury claims —
   that's the pilot phase and beyond."

Q: "Where does your 25-30% cost reduction come from?"
A: "It's an estimate, labeled as such: cost = agent time × hourly
   rate; our measured -80% declaration time plus a 40-60%
   auto-approval eligibility hypothesis on sub-500-dinar claims.
   Industry studies on claims automation land in the same range.
   The pilot's first deliverable is replacing this estimate with
   a measured number."

Q: "Why no revenue forecast / IRR?"
A: "Because it would be fiction. Our unit economics depend on one
   number we can't know yet: the real auto-approval rate on real
   claims. The pilot measures it; the day we have it, the IRR is
   arithmetic. We prefer one honest unknown to five invented
   certainties."

Q: "Why would an insurer trust automated decisions?"
A: "Our AI is constrained by construction: it can only
   auto-approve when amount, confidence AND fraud score are all
   green — otherwise a human decides. Every automated decision is
   persisted with the gate that produced it and the weighted
   factors behind it. That's an audit trail a regulator can read."

Q: "Where does the data live?"
A: "Identity documents and selfies are processed locally by our
   own service — they never go to a cloud AI provider. Only claim
   photos and document text go to external AI APIs. On-premise
   deployment at the insurer is the target topology."

Q: "How is this different from Tractable / Shift?"
A: "Scope, language, sovereignty. They automate one link for
   mature markets, in the cloud. We cover eKYC plus claims in one
   journey, in derja, with Law 2015-26 native, deployable inside
   the insurer's walls. Different market, different promise."

Q: "What's the moat?"
A: "The reusable-KYC network: one CIN equals one verified
   dossier, portable between member insurers. Every insurer that
   joins makes the next one's onboarding cheaper — compounding a
   foreign point-solution can't easily replicate."

DELIVERY TIPS
================================================================
- 11 slides in 4:00 is FAST: some slides get 10 seconds — that's
  normal in Kawasaki format; the slide shows, you say one line.
- Cut-list if over time: trim S2 (the hook carries it), then S8
  to one sentence. NEVER cut the demo (S4) or honesty of S9.
- Slide 4 rule from the template: figures and schemas, not text.
  Put the 3-gates diagram, nothing else.
- Pre-load the demo: chat open, photos ready, logged in. Record a
  30s backup video in case Wi-Fi dies.
- One speaker for the pitch; switch speakers only at Q&A.
- Eye contact on the jury during S2 (hook) and S11 (ask) — read
  NOTHING on those two.
- End exactly on: "insured in five minutes, declared in three,
  decided with control."
