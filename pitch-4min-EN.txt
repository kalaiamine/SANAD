================================================================
SANAD — 4-MINUTE PITCH SCRIPT (English)
Structure: WORKSHOP BACKBONE
  Hook (custom) → 1. Problem → 2. Solution (+30s demo)
  → 3. Business model → Traction & Impact (custom)
  → 4. Team → Risks (custom) → 5. Call to action
Format: 4 min pitch + 4 min Q&A · ~500 spoken words + 30s demo
================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------
HOOK  (0:00 – 0:20)   [custom 15% — "tell a story: the why"]
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Last month, a driver in Sfax had a small accident. A dented
bumper, nothing more. It took him three weeks, two trips to an
agency, and forms in a language he doesn't live in, to get an
answer. Three weeks... for a bumper. That driver is why we built
SANAD."

[Pause one beat. Let it land.]

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PROBLEM  (0:20 – 0:50)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"His story is the Tunisian insurance market in miniature. KYC is
paper-based, in-branch, and every insurer makes you redo it from
zero. Declaring a claim means fifteen to twenty minutes of forms,
office hours only, then days of manual processing. Forms are in
French, while the client's real language is derja. And the check
between what you declare and what your photo shows barely exists.
Insurers lose money on every small claim; honest clients wait."

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. SOLUTION + 30-SECOND DEMO  (0:50 – 1:55)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY (25s):
"SANAD is one platform for the full journey — identity to
indemnity. Verify your identity once: ID scan, selfie with
anti-spoofing, AML screening aligned with Law 2015-26 — processed
locally, so identity data can stay inside the insurer's walls.
Then declare claims 24/7, in French, Arabic, or derja. And one
rule above all: our AI may only auto-approve a claim when three
lights are green — small amount, high confidence, low fraud risk.
Otherwise a human expert decides. Let me show you — this runs."

DEMO (30 seconds, pre-loaded, rehearsed):
  1. Chat open: type the derja message — "sadamt fi 9alb el
     mdina" — the bot replies in derja, claim fields fill in.
  2. Upload the damage photo → repair estimate in dinars.
  3. THE MONEY SHOT: the decision banner — green "auto-approved",
     or amber "routed to a human expert", with the reason.

SAY (10s, over the banner):
"Automation when the AI is sure, a human when it doubts — and
every decision is stored with the factors behind it. That is an
audit trail a regulator can read."

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. BUSINESS MODEL  (1:55 – 2:20)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"We sell SaaS to insurers: a fee per verified identity, a fee per
processed claim. The verified identity is reusable — one CIN, one
KYC, portable between member insurers. Each insurer that joins
makes the next one's onboarding cheaper. That network effect is
our long-term defensibility."

----------------------------------------------------------------
TRACTION & IMPACT  (2:20 – 2:55)   [custom 15% — honest numbers]
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"What exists today, measured on the product you just saw:
declaration time down from fifteen-plus minutes to about three.
Identity verified in five minutes. Eighty-two percent OCR
confidence on a real Tunisian ID. Forty-six automated tests,
green, on a public repository. What we estimate — and we label it
as an estimate: if forty to sixty percent of small claims pass
our three gates, processing costs drop twenty-five to thirty
percent, in line with published studies on claims automation.
The pilot exists to turn that estimate into a measurement."

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. TEAM  (2:55 – 3:10)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY (adapt names/roles):
"We are [names/roles]. In 48 hours we shipped the full journey —
two AI services, tests, one-command deployment. We execute."

----------------------------------------------------------------
RISKS — ACKNOWLEDGED  (3:10 – 3:30)   [custom 15% — address risks]
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"We know our risks. Competitors like Tractable and Shift have
deeper models on their single link — our edge is scope, language
and data sovereignty, not out-modeling them. Our thresholds are
hypotheses until calibrated on real claims data. And regulatory
adoption takes a partner, not a promise. That is exactly what our
pilot is designed to de-risk."

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. CALL TO ACTION  (3:30 – 4:00)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"So here is our ask: one Tunisian insurer for a pilot on small
auto claims, access to anonymized data to calibrate our
thresholds, and regulatory guidance. Remember the driver from
Sfax — three weeks for a bumper. With SANAD: insured in five
minutes, declared in three, decided with control. Join us. Thank
you."

WORKSHOP TIPS — HOW THIS SCRIPT APPLIES THEM
================================================================
✔ Avoid overpromising   → impact split MEASURED vs ESTIMATED,
                          estimate explicitly labeled.
✔ Acknowledge competition → Tractable/Shift named, their strength
                          admitted ("deeper models"), our edge scoped.
✔ Skip superlatives     → no "revolutionary/best/unique" anywhere;
                          facts and numbers instead.
✔ Address risks         → dedicated 20-second section (rare in
                          hackathon pitches — juries notice).
✔ Keep it simple        → one idea per section, no tech jargon
                          (no "SHAP/LLM/YOLO" in the spoken text).
✔ Highlight benefits    → 5 min / 3 min / decision on the spot,
                          repeated at hook and close.
✔ Tell a story (the why)→ the Sfax driver opens AND closes (loop).
✔ Involve the audience  → "Remember the driver from Sfax" +
                          the live demo moment.
✔ Practice & customize  → timing per section, cut-list below.

Q&A PREPARATION (4 minutes — anticipated questions)
================================================================

Q: "Too ambitious for a hackathon — what actually works?"
A: "Everything we demoed runs end-to-end: Arabic ID OCR, AML
   screening, liveness, derja chat, photo damage estimate, gated
   decision, database persistence. 46 automated tests are green.
   What is NOT in the MVP is deliberately listed: insurer system
   integration, real payouts, complex bodily-injury claims —
   that's Phase 2 and 3."

Q: "Where does your 25-30% cost reduction come from?"
A: "It's an estimate, labeled as such: cost = agent time × hourly
   rate; our measured -80% declaration time plus a 40-60%
   auto-approval eligibility hypothesis on sub-500-dinar claims.
   Industry studies land in the same range. The pilot's first
   deliverable is replacing this estimate with a measured number."

Q: "Why would an insurer trust automated decisions?"
A: "Our AI is constrained by construction: it can only
   auto-approve when amount, confidence AND fraud score are all
   green — otherwise a human decides. Every automated decision is
   persisted with the gate that produced it and the weighted
   factors behind it. That's an audit trail a regulator can read."

Q: "Where does the data live?"
A: "Identity documents and selfies are processed locally by our
   own service — they never go to a cloud AI provider. Only claim
   photos and document text go to external AI APIs. On-premise
   deployment at the insurer is the target topology."

Q: "How is this different from Tractable / Shift?"
A: "Scope, language, sovereignty. They automate one link for
   mature markets, in the cloud. We cover eKYC plus claims in one
   journey, in derja, with Law 2015-26 native, deployable inside
   the insurer's walls. Different market, different promise."

Q: "What's the moat?"
A: "The reusable-KYC network: one CIN equals one verified
   dossier, portable between member insurers. Every insurer that
   joins makes the next one's onboarding cheaper — compounding a
   foreign point-solution can't easily replicate."

================================================================
DELIVERY TIPS
================================================================
- 4:00 is BRUTAL. Rehearse with a timer. Cut-list if over:
  first trim PROBLEM (the hook carries it), then TEAM to one
  sentence. Never cut the demo or the risks section.
- Hook and close mirror each other (Sfax driver) — learn both by
  heart, never improvise them.
- Pre-load the demo: chat open, photos ready, logged in. Record a
  30s backup video in case Wi-Fi dies.
- One speaker for the pitch; switch speakers only at Q&A.
- Eye contact on the jury during the hook and the ask — read
  NOTHING on those two sections.
- During the demo, say only the scripted lines — let the decision
  banner do the talking.
- End exactly on: "insured in five minutes, declared in three,
  decided with control. Join us."
