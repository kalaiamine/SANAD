================================================================
SANAD — 4-MINUTE PITCH SCRIPT (English)
Format: Kawasaki 10-slide structure · 4 min pitch + 4 min Q&A
Includes a 30-second live demo slot (Slide 4)
Target pace: ~140 words/min → ~500 spoken words + 30s demo
================================================================

Jury criteria this script is built around:
  30% Real problem & Tunisia anchoring → Slides 2-3
  25% Technical solidity & AI feasibility → Slides 4 + demo (it runs!)
  15% Innovation & differentiation → Slides 4 & 7
  15% Viability & scale → Slides 5, 6, 9
  15% Quality & honesty of numbers → Slide 9 (measured vs estimated)

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 1 — TITLE  (0:00 – 0:15)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Good afternoon. We are SANAD. In Tunisia, opening an insurance
account takes days of paperwork — and settling a car claim takes
weeks. We built a platform where you verify your identity in five
minutes and declare an accident in three — in French, in Arabic,
or in Tunisian derja."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 2 — PROBLEM / OPPORTUNITY  (0:15 – 0:45)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Three pain points, all measured on the ground. One: KYC is done
in-branch, on paper, and every insurer makes you redo it. Two: a
traditional claim declaration means fifteen to twenty minutes of
form-filling, office hours only, then days of processing. Three:
the forms are in French — but the language our clients actually
live in is derja. And fraud checking between the photo and the
declaration? In practice, it barely exists."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 3 — VALUE PROPOSITION  (0:45 – 1:05)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"SANAD unifies the two most expensive processes in insurance into
one journey: verify your identity once — ID card scan, selfie with
anti-spoofing, AML screening fully aligned with Tunisian Law
2015-26 — then declare claims 24/7 by simply talking to an
assistant that understands you, whatever language you speak."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 4 — THE UNDERLYING MAGIC + 30-SECOND DEMO  (1:05 – 1:50)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY (10s, then run the demo):
"Here is our core innovation — and it's running live, not a
mock-up. Watch the decision gate."

DEMO (30 seconds, pre-loaded, rehearsed):
  1. Chat already open: message in derja — "sadamt fi 9alb el
     mdina" — bot answers in derja, claim fields fill themselves.
  2. Damage photo uploaded → cost estimate in dinars appears.
  3. THE MONEY SHOT: the decision banner. Green "auto-approved"
     — or amber "routed to a human expert" with the reason shown.

SAY (5s, over the banner):
"Our AI doesn't just explain its decisions. It knows when it is
NOT allowed to decide: low confidence or fraud risk means a human
takes over. Automation under control."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 5 — BUSINESS MODEL  (1:50 – 2:10)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"We sell SaaS to insurers: a per-verified-identity fee for eKYC,
and a per-processed-claim fee. The reusable KYC is the network
effect: a client verified once can be accepted by another member
insurer without redoing the process — the more insurers join, the
more valuable each verified identity becomes."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 6 — GO-TO-MARKET  (2:10 – 2:30)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Entry point: one Tunisian insurer, one pilot on small auto
claims — under 500 dinars — where automation is safest and the
volume is highest. The pilot calibrates our thresholds on real
data. Then we expand claim types, then insurers — toward a shared
KYC utility for the whole Tunisian market."

----------------------------------------------------------------

----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Tractable estimates vehicle damage. Shift Technology detects

cloud, at enterprise prices, with no derja. SANAD covers the FULL
journey — identity to indemnity — with identity data processed
locally, Tunisian law built in, and a cost structure sized for
Maghreb insurers. We don't out-model them; we out-cover them,
here."

----------------------------------------------------------------

Q&A PREPARATION (4 minutes — anticipated questions)
================================================================

Q: "Too ambitious for a hackathon — what actually works?"
A: "Everything we demoed runs end-to-end: ID OCR in Arabic, AML
   screening, liveness, derja chat, photo damage estimate, gated
   decision, database persistence. 46 automated tests are green.
   What is NOT in the MVP is deliberately listed: insurer system
   integration, real payouts, complex bodily-injury claims —
   that's Phase 2 and 3."

Q: "Where does your 25-30% cost reduction come from?"
A: "It's an estimate, labeled as such: cost = agent time × hourly
   rate; our measured -80% declaration time plus a 40-60%
   auto-approval eligibility hypothesis on sub-500-dinar claims.
   Industry studies on claims automation land in the same range.
   The pilot's first deliverable is replacing this estimate with
   a measured number."

Q: "Why would an insurer trust automated decisions?"
A: "Because our AI is constrained by construction: it can only
   auto-approve when amount, confidence, AND fraud score are all
   green — otherwise a human decides. Every automated decision is
   persisted with the gate that produced it and the weighted
   factors behind it. That's an audit trail a regulator can read."

Q: "What about data protection / where does the data live?"
A: "Identity documents and selfies are processed locally by our
   own service — they never go to a cloud AI provider. Only claim
   photos and document text go to external AI APIs. Passwords are
   hashed, sessions are signed, roles are enforced server-side.
   On-premise deployment at the insurer is our target topology."

Q: "How is this different from Tractable / Shift?"
A: "Scope, language, and sovereignty. They each automate one link
   for mature markets, in the cloud. We cover eKYC plus claims in
   one journey, in derja, with Law 2015-26 compliance native, and
   identity processing that can run inside the insurer's walls."

Q: "What's the moat?"
A: "The reusable-KYC network: one CIN equals one verified dossier,
   portable between member insurers. Every insurer that joins
   makes the next one's onboarding cheaper — that compounding is
   hard for a foreign point-solution to replicate."

DELIVERY TIPS
================================================================
- 4:00 is BRUTAL. Rehearse with a timer; cut Slide 8 to one
  sentence if you're over.
- Pre-load the demo: chat open, photos in a desktop folder,
  logged in. Record a 30s backup video in case Wi-Fi dies.
- One speaker for the pitch; switch speakers only at Q&A.
- During the demo, say NOTHING except the two scripted lines —
  let the decision banner do the talking.
- End exactly on the tagline: "insure in five minutes, declare in
  three, decide with control."
