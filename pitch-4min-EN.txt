SANAD — 4-MINUTE PITCH SCRIPT (English)
Structure: HOOK → PROBLEM → SOLUTION → METHODOLOGY → RESULTS → CONCLUSION
Format: 4 min pitch + 4 min Q&A · includes a 30-second live demo
Target pace: ~140 words/min → ~500 spoken words + 30s demo
================================================================

Jury criteria this script is built around:
  30% Real problem & Tunisia anchoring → PROBLEM
  25% Technical solidity & AI feasibility → METHODOLOGY + DEMO (it runs!)
  15% Innovation & differentiation → SOLUTION + METHODOLOGY
  15% Viability & scale → RESULTS + CONCLUSION
  15% Quality & honesty of numbers → RESULTS (measured vs estimated)

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. CAPTIVATING HOOK  (0:00 – 0:20)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Last month, a driver in Sfax had a small accident. Nothing
serious — a dented bumper. It took him three weeks, two trips to
an agency, and forms in a language he doesn't live in, to get an
answer. Three weeks... for a bumper. We fixed that. With SANAD,
he is verified in five minutes, declares in three — speaking
Tunisian derja — and gets a decision on the spot."

[Pause one beat. Let "three weeks for a bumper" land.]

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE PROBLEM  (0:20 – 0:55)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"This story is the Tunisian insurance market in miniature. Three
structural failures. One: KYC is paper-based, in-branch, and
every insurer makes you redo it from zero. Two: declaring a claim
means fifteen to twenty minutes of forms, office hours only, then
days of manual processing. Three: everything is in French, while
the client's real language is derja — and the fraud check between
what you declare and what the photo shows barely exists. Insurers
lose money on every small claim; honest clients wait; fraudsters
walk through."

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE SOLUTION  (0:55 – 1:25)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"SANAD is one platform for the full journey — from identity to
indemnity. You verify your identity once: ID card scan, selfie
with anti-spoofing, AML screening aligned with Tunisian Law
2015-26. That verified identity becomes a reusable asset — one
CIN, one KYC, portable between insurers. Then you declare claims
24/7 by talking to an assistant in French, Arabic, or derja. And
our key innovation: the AI's explainability is not a display —
it is a DECISION GATE. The AI may only auto-approve when three
lights are green: small amount, high confidence, low fraud risk.
Otherwise, a human expert decides."

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. METHODOLOGY + 30-SECOND DEMO  (1:25 – 2:20)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY (15s):
"How it works, concretely. Identity documents are processed
LOCALLY — Arabic OCR and face-match never leave the server. The
conversation and document reading use a large language model with
structured output. Damage photos go through a vision model
trained on car damage, priced against a Tunisian dinar cost grid.
Every step is written to a timestamped audit trail. Let me show
you — this is live, not a mock-up."

DEMO (30 seconds, pre-loaded, rehearsed):
  1. Chat open: type the derja message — "sadamt fi 9alb el
     mdina" — the bot replies in derja, claim fields fill in.
  2. Upload the damage photo → repair estimate in dinars appears.
  3. THE MONEY SHOT: the decision banner — green "auto-approved",
     or amber "routed to a human expert", with the reason.

SAY (10s, over the banner):
"That banner is the methodology in one image: automation when the
AI is sure, a human when it doubts — and every decision stored
with the factors that produced it. That's what a regulator can
audit."

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. RESULTS — MEASURED vs ESTIMATED  (2:20 – 3:10)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Our results, in two honest columns. MEASURED, on the product you
just saw: declaration time down from fifteen-plus minutes to
about three — minus eighty percent. Identity verified in five
minutes instead of a branch visit. Eighty-two percent OCR
confidence on a real Tunisian ID. Forty-six automated tests,
green, on a public repository, deployable with one command.
ESTIMATED — and we label it as such: with our cost formula, if
forty to sixty percent of sub-500-dinar claims pass the three
gates, claims processing costs drop twenty-five to thirty
percent. That range matches published industry studies on claims
automation. We refuse to sell you that estimate as a fact — the
pilot exists to turn it into a measurement."

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. CONCLUSION + ASK  (3:10 – 4:00)
----------------------------------------------------------------
SAY:
"Where competitors like Tractable or Shift automate one link of
the chain, in the cloud, at enterprise prices and without derja —
SANAD covers the whole journey, in the client's language, with
identity data that can stay inside the insurer's walls, and
Tunisian law built in. What we ask for today: one insurer pilot
on small auto claims, access to anonymized data to calibrate our
thresholds, and regulatory guidance. Remember the driver from
Sfax: three weeks for a bumper. With SANAD: insured in five
minutes, declared in three, decided with control. Thank you."

Q&A PREPARATION (4 minutes — anticipated questions)
================================================================

Q: "Too ambitious for a hackathon — what actually works?"
A: "Everything we demoed runs end-to-end: ID OCR in Arabic, AML
   screening, liveness, derja chat, photo damage estimate, gated
   decision, database persistence. 46 automated tests are green.
   What is NOT in the MVP is deliberately listed: insurer system
   integration, real payouts, complex bodily-injury claims —
   that's Phase 2 and 3."

Q: "Where does your 25-30% cost reduction come from?"
A: "It's an estimate, labeled as such: cost = agent time × hourly
   rate; our measured -80% declaration time plus a 40-60%
   auto-approval eligibility hypothesis on sub-500-dinar claims.
   Industry studies on claims automation land in the same range.
   The pilot's first deliverable is replacing this estimate with
   a measured number."

Q: "Why would an insurer trust automated decisions?"
A: "Because our AI is constrained by construction: it can only
   auto-approve when amount, confidence, AND fraud score are all
   green — otherwise a human decides. Every automated decision is
   persisted with the gate that produced it and the weighted
   factors behind it. That's an audit trail a regulator can read."

Q: "What about data protection / where does the data live?"
A: "Identity documents and selfies are processed locally by our
   own service — they never go to a cloud AI provider. Only claim
   photos and document text go to external AI APIs. Passwords are
   hashed, sessions are signed, roles are enforced server-side.
   On-premise deployment at the insurer is our target topology."

Q: "How is this different from Tractable / Shift?"
A: "Scope, language, and sovereignty. They each automate one link
   for mature markets, in the cloud. We cover eKYC plus claims in
   one journey, in derja, with Law 2015-26 compliance native, and
   identity processing that can run inside the insurer's walls."

Q: "What's the moat?"
A: "The reusable-KYC network: one CIN equals one verified dossier,
   portable between member insurers. Every insurer that joins
   makes the next one's onboarding cheaper — that compounding is
   hard for a foreign point-solution to replicate."

================================================================
DELIVERY TIPS
================================================================
- 4:00 is BRUTAL. Rehearse with a timer. If you run over, trim
  the PROBLEM section first (the hook already carries it).
- The hook and the conclusion mirror each other (the Sfax driver)
  — do not improvise them, learn them by heart.
- Pre-load the demo: chat open, photos in a desktop folder,
  logged in. Record a 30s backup video in case Wi-Fi dies.
- One speaker for the pitch; switch speakers only at Q&A.
- During the demo, say NOTHING except the scripted lines — let
  the decision banner do the talking.
- End exactly on the tagline: "insured in five minutes, declared
  in three, decided with control."
